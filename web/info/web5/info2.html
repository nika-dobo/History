The Memory Wars

By 2789, memories were no longer private. Thanks to the NeuroKey implants, people could store, sell, or share memories like digital files. The rich curated perfect pasts, while the poor leased their best memories to survive.But everything changed when a terrorist group known as The Wipers attacked the Central Memory Bank in orbit. In one night, millions of people forgot who they were — families collapsed, governments dissolved, and society fractured into “Pre-Keepers” (those with backups) and “Nulls” (those with nothing).Tariq Elen, once a memory engineer, is now a Null, wandering across fragmented city-states where people forge fake memories just to be “someone.” He meets Lune, a mysterious girl who claims she remembers everything — even events no one else does.Together, they uncover that the memory crash wasn’t a mistake. It was part of a corporate experiment to manipulate reality by controlling collective memory. The real war wasn’t for land or power, but for truth itself.In the final chapters, Tariq and Lune lead a revolution to restore lost memory fragments and build a world where identity isn’t manufactured
