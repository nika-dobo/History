The Mind Orchard

By 3299, people no longer store memories in their brains. Instead, they grow Memory Orchards — living gardens of neuro-organic trees, each fruit containing a single memory or dream. When someone wants to remember something, they eat a fruit and relive it fully.Juno Vale, a memory-farmer, manages orchards for high-level thinkers and artists. But one day, she bites into a fruit that doesn’t belong to her — a black pear hidden among golden apples. In it, she sees a future that hasn’t happened yet: a vision of the Earth on fire, cities collapsed, and a voice whispering, “This is your fault.”Juno becomes obsessed with finding the origin of this memory. She follows a trail of clues across the galaxy’s cultural centers — from underwater music monasteries to Martian memory auctions — uncovering a hidden conspiracy. The Memory Network had begun planting false futures into influential minds, shaping reality not through force, but through fear.In the end, Juno must decide: erase the corrupted trees, risking mass amnesia, or let the illusion continue and live in a beautiful lie.
