Subconscious Voting

In 2491, democracy had been replaced by Dream-Based Governance. Through a system called MindVote, political decisions were made not by spoken opinion, but by subconscious desires detected during sleep. The theory was simple: people lie when awake, but their dreams reveal their true beliefs.While asleep, citizens entered a deep neural state monitored by AI. Their thoughts, memories, fears, and hopes were analyzed to vote on laws, leaders, and global policies.Miko Sarinava, an independent scientist, discovered a flaw — or perhaps a horror. People's votes were not purely subconscious; they were shaped by childhood trauma, propaganda, and subliminal advertising. The very foundation of MindVote was a manipulation engine.To prove this, Miko assembled a team of lucid dreamers and dove into the collective subconscious — a place known as The Commons. What they found was terrifying: political monsters shaped from social media algorithms, fears manufactured by AI, and false memories planted by corporate interests.Miko proposes a new system called The Empathy Protocol, where decision-making is based on biometric empathy responses, not unconscious bias. At the final vote — conducted both in dreams and in reality — the world must choose: continue with the illusion of perfect democracy or return to a flawed, but conscious, human governance.
